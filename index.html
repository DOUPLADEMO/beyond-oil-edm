Research

What the science says about frying oil

Frying oil that's heated and reheated builds up polar compounds — the chemical signature of oxidation. Most regulators across Europe set a hard ceiling around 24% TPC, the point at which oil is considered unfit for food preparation.

Past that ceiling, repeatedly heated oil also accumulates aldehydes, including 4-HNE, which are linked in peer-reviewed work to oxidative stress and chronic inflammation. It's not just a quality problem — it's a health problem.

"With Beyond Oil, the safe polar-compound range is extended roughly 4× — meaning oil stays well below regulatory thresholds far longer in the same fryer."

The result downstream is what your guests actually taste: crispier food, lighter color, a cleaner finish. The result upstream is fewer oil changes, less smoke, and measurably better air for the people on the line.
